# Cow Alert — How It Works

## The Problem It Solves

When a cow gets loose, the only option right now is for a resident to track down a phone number and make a call. That takes time — and in the meantime, the cow is still out there. There's also no way for anyone to know if the issue has already been reported or if someone is already on the way.

Cow Alert fixes all of that.

---

## What It Is

Cow Alert is a simple website that lets residents of Laureate Park report a loose cow in under 30 seconds, straight from their phone. No app to download, no account to create. They open a browser, tap a button, and the report is sent.

You get a text message the moment a report comes in.

---

## How a Report Works (Resident Side)

1. A resident spots a loose cow and opens the website on their phone.
2. They tap **"Report a Loose Cow"**.
3. They take a quick photo of the cow. The app automatically checks that there's actually a cow in the photo before the report can go through — no false alarms from someone sending a blurry picture of their dog.
4. The app uses their phone's GPS to detect where they are and suggests the nearest known location on your property. They can confirm it or pick a different spot from a list.
5. They can add a short description if they want, then hit submit.
6. Done. The report is in, and you get an email. (Future version maybe text)

The whole thing takes less time than finding your number and dialing it.

---

## What You Get (Rancher Side)

The moment a report is submitted, you receive an email with the location and any description the resident provided.

You also have access to a private **dashboard** where you can:

- See all active reports at a glance, organized by location
- Mark a report as **Acknowledged** (so residents know you're aware and on the way)
- Mark a report as **Resolved** once the cow is back where it belongs
- Review past reports if needed

No complicated setup — just log in and everything is right there.

---

## What Residents See

On the public side of the website, residents can see whether any active reports are already open. This means if someone has already reported the same cow, the next person who spots it will see that the ranger has already been notified — cutting down on repeat calls and duplicate reports. To further prevent duplicates, users cannot submit a new report from a location that already has an active alert.

They see the status update in real time as you acknowledge and resolve reports, so they know someone is handling it without having to wonder.

---

## The Bottom Line

- Residents report faster than they could make a phone call
- You get notified instantly, every time
- No duplicate reports and unnecessary calls
- You can see everything in one place and update the status as you handle it
- Everyone stays in the loop without anyone having to coordinate by phone
